Export Logistics & Container Capability

Container Loading Optimization & Density Engineering

Sourcing dense FMCG products like laundry detergents requires balancing the physical volume of a container against legal road weight limits in the destination country (such as the U.S. Federal Bridge Law limit of 80,000 lbs gross weight, restricting internal cargo payloads to approximately 19,958 kg).

The practical volumetric utilization (U) of each shipping container is calculated as follows:
U = ( ( ∑ V_carton,i ) / V_container ) × 100
Practical usable container volume is calculated at 85% of theoretical maximum to account for dunnage and structural bracing.

Compliance Readiness Framework

Frictionless Western Customs Clearance Protocols

We engineer our products backward from the regulatory boundaries of our target destination markets. Our formulations and packaging setups are structured to support Western chemical, ecological, and consumer health safety mandates.

United States Market Stream

  • TSCA Alignment: Core active surfactants, including Linear Alkylbenzene Sulfonic Acid (LABSA), are verified on the EPA's Toxic Substances Control Act inventory.
  • Standard 16-Section SDS: Pre-compiled, OSHA-compliant Safety Data Sheets are ready for warehousing and transport clearance.
  • Proposition 65 Exclusions: Formulas are screened to remain below California Prop 65 reporting thresholds, eliminating warning label mandates.

European & UK Market Stream

  • Aerobic Biodegradability: Surfactants satisfy biodegradability parameters defined under current European and UK Detergents Regulations.
  • REACH & CLP Alignment: Formulations support classification and chemical notifications via ECHA portal parameters.
  • Digital Passport Integration: Structured to integrate Unique Formula Identifiers (UFI Codes) and align with upcoming Digital Product Passport (DPP) data metrics.
